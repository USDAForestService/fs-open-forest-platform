Green (live) Oak Brush may be cut if the trees are less than 6 inches in diameter measured at the root collar (near the ground level). This diameter is equivalent to 19 inches in circumference measured around the tree bole at the root collar.

DO NOT CUT GREEN TREES unless in specifically designated areas. DO NOT CUT trees marked with paint, plastic ribbon and/or signs. DO NOT CUT trees that have bird cavities (holes), nests, or are marked with wildlife signs.

DO NOT CUT wildlife trees. Wildlife trees/snags are trees that have been designated as an asset to some kinds of wild Animals. Wildlife trees will have remnants of marking paing, ribbon or have a "Wildlife Tree" sign.

The following are also NOT available for fuelwood cutting:

  • Live/green trees (excluding gamble oak) unless the tree is blown down, and the roots are exposed. A live tree is defined as a tree with any amount of green needles or foliage.
  • Standing dead ponderosa pine trees that are 18 inches or larger in diameter. These trees provide much needed wildlife habitat.
  • Piles or "decks' of logs constructed by loggers. These decks are from timber sales that have been purchased from the Forest Service by the logger. Cutting fuelwood from log decks is timber theft.
  • Fuelwood cutting within designated wilderness areas is allowed for on-site use only, however, chainsaws and motorized equipment are not allowed in the wilderness areas.
  • Trees from administrative sites including guard stations, campgrounds and picnic areas.
